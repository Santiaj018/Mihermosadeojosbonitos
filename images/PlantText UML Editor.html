Feb 1, 2024 - PlantText Version 21 Released!

Thanks to everyone who has been supporting and donating to PlantText.com

Here is a short list of new features...

  • Editor and image areas have been enlarged!
  • The toolbar is now vertical and draggable
  • Improvements to the Samples window
  • Users can automatically Save and Refresh the diagram
  • Automatically scale the diagram image to fit your screen
  • The Theme Chooser is now in the Settings window

Read about the lastest features!

If you are new to PlantText, take a look below to get started...

You are currently looking at the News window. Clicking the News button to the right brings up this window. When there is something note worthy, you will see it here. But right now you see this helpful little tutorial.

Next you will want to click the Samples button to work with hundreds of plantuml diagrams. Select a category and then a specific diagram. Notice the PlantUML and resulting diagram appear below. Take a look and add it to the editor if you want to work with it.

The Settings button now has all sorts of interesting options. Spend some time trying them all out. The Diagram Theme dropdown is a great way to change the look of a diagram quickly. Snippets are great too. And don't forget that Alt + Enter is a quick keystroke for refreshing your diagram.

Delete Profileclose

When you create a user profile in PlantText, we only store your authentication information on our servers. All of your data (settings and diagrams) is stored in your browser's local storage, NOT our server. If you are wanting to delete the local data, please go to the settings window.

Warning! This CANNOT be undone. You will need to register again if you wish to login to PlantText. Your data will not best lost by deleting this account because it is stored locally, but your user profile will be deleted from our server.

Link Makerclose

Convert raw PlantUML text into a URL. The URL can then be copied into a browser in order to create the diagram image. This URL is useful when trying to put PlantUML diagrams into documents or uploading them to other websites.
